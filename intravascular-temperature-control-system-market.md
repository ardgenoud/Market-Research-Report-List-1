<p><p>The intravascular temperature control system market encompasses two primary types: intravascular warming and intravascular cooling. Intravascular warming systems are designed to elevate a patient's core temperature during procedures or in cases of hypothermia, enhancing recovery and reducing complications. Conversely, intravascular cooling systems aim to lower body temperature for therapeutic reasons, such as managing fever or protecting brain function during cardiac arrest. Both systems are critical in hospital settings for optimizing patient outcomes through precise temperature regulation.</p></p>
