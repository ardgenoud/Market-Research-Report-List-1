<p><p>The vaccine delivery market comprises various types, including biological vectors, chemical carriers, and others. Biological vectors utilize living organisms, such as viruses or bacteria, to transport vaccines directly into cells, enhancing immune responses. Chemical carriers involve synthetic or natural compounds that stabilize and facilitate the absorption of vaccines into the body, improving efficacy. Other methods may include microneedles, nanoparticles, or lipid-based systems that optimize delivery mechanisms. Each type aims to enhance vaccine effectiveness, safety, and patient compliance.</p></p>
