<p><p>HMOs, or human milk oligosaccharides, are classified into two market types: neutral HMOs and acidic HMOs. Neutral HMOs are primarily composed of non-sialylated structures and are abundant in breast milk, playing a crucial role in gut health and the immune system of infants. Acidic HMOs, on the other hand, contain sialic acid and are associated with enhanced cognitive development and protection against infections. Both types are increasingly recognized for their potential in infant nutrition and functional food applications.</p></p>
