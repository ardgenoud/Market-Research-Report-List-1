<p><p>The Aluminum Alloy Automotive Wheels market is primarily divided into two types: forging and casting. Forged aluminum alloy wheels are created through a process that involves shaping the metal under high pressure, resulting in a stronger, lighter product with superior performance characteristics. In contrast, casting aluminum alloy wheels are produced by pouring molten aluminum into molds, allowing for intricate designs and cost-effective manufacturing. Both types offer distinct advantages in terms of weight, aesthetics, and durability, catering to diverse automotive needs.</p></p>
